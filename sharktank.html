Fictional Product Launch Competition

Background

This was the final project in the Creativity In Marketing course I took in fall of 2015. The charge was to pitch a new product designed to appeal to one of three assigned minority target markets: Hispanic and Latino American, black Americans, and LGBT Americans. The winning group from each of the three target demographics then proceeded to a department-wide "shark tank" style event, which was held in an auditorium with guest judges from outside of the university. My group was selected from the Hispanic and Latino American sub-demographic as a finalist for the competition.

Coffee Cabeza

My group's idea was a line of coffee products inspired by the artwork, political climate, and distinct flavor profiles found along the coffee belt in Central America. The countries we chose to base our three lines of beans on were Cuba, Mexico, and Puerto Rico. By associating our products with humanitarian and politcal issues, we sought to capture the educated and activist millennial demographic. The packaging design was based on art styles historically found in those three countries. I was responsible for creating the presentation, presenting, and designing the products. You can view the full presentation here.

Result

My group ultimately won the shark tank competition and were commended for talking directly to our target market and by going the extra mile by drawing inspiration from the historic art styles of the three countries we were sampling. The project was a great experience because it forced me to market to a demographic with which I am not familiar. I had to consider the product from an entirely different perspective than my own, which is crucial for any new product development process.
